Artists’ Intended Message

“(Un)veil” is about obsession,
“Threads of Obscurity” is not.

The painting shown was chosen at random. It was not intended to show any message, with the only interpretation of the ‘art’ coming from the audience.

The curated experience of the exhibition is our actual artwork, with the painting having a supporting role. With the (un)veil, we wanted to bring across this idea of ‘obsession with art’.

See how we reproduced the curtain into an 'artwork'

It is meant to simulate an curatorial experience that would lead to the audience contributing their thoughts on the ‘painting’. We discovered a range of responses, from in-depth analyses to direct visual descriptions.

It brings us to our message that sometimes an artwork is assigned more meaning than what an artist originally intended for it to have, based on one’s own ideals and interpretation of what ‘art’ is.
